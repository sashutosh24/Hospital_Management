Laboratory Medicine


 

Dr L H Hiranandani Hospital has a "round-the-clock" laboratory testing facility spread over 2500 square feet, located in the first floor with additional analytical area in the eighth floor. The Department of Laboratory Medicine is equipped with the latest state-of-art analyzers, which are fully integrated with the laboratory information system (LIS) providing for online patient results. The department boasts of consistent and quality results through stringent quality control measures and participation in external proficiency testing programmes. The department has been accreditated in the field of medical testing by the National Accreditation Board for Testing and Calibration Laboratories (NABL), Department of Science and Technology (Government of India) since 2008. 


Salient Features
• The laboratory provides holistic services in the form of Clinical Pathology, Microbiology, Histopathology, Biochemistry, Haematology and Immunoserology. 

• 24-hour operation provides for the any test, any time approach to the patient management. 

• Automated state-of-art equipments (details provided below)

• Integrated Laboratory Information System: The system integration also provides for the capability of online reporting thus reducing time of turn around of results. 

• Bar-code (positive) identification system: The system improves the efficiency of the process flow and minimises any random errors. 


• Bi-directional interface of analyzers: This ensures error-free reporting and improves the turnaround time by obviating manual entry. 

• Web-report viewing: The system facilitates the patients to view their result using the internet at their base. This result viewing is password protected. 

• Quality Assurance programmes: The laboratory conducts stringent quality assurance programmes using internal quality controls as well as robust quality measures in place. The department is participant to various proficiency testing programmes conducted by AIIMS, CMC Vellore, Sankara Nethralaya & international programmes of Biorad EQAS, thus helping in validating its quality process. 

• NABL Accreditation: The Laboratory is accreditated for the medical testing facility in the field of medical testing by the NABL (Certificate no M-0135) since 2008, one of the first hospital-based laboratory to be accredited in Mumbai. 

• Home-care facilities: For patient convenience, we also provide personalized services in the form of home collection of blood samples. 

• Stat sample facilities: As part of our endeavour to improve the patient management, the laboratory has STAT facilities where the urgent samples are processed and reported on priority. 

• Qualified and well-trained team of pathologists, histopathologists and microbiologists alongwith technologists is the cornerstone of the laboratory services.

Clinical Biochemistry
The Biochemistry section is equipped with the latest dry chemistry technology from the Ortho-Clinical Diagnostic (Johnson & Johnson). TheVitros 350 analyzers are equipped with primary tube sampling with sample identification via bar code scanner system. The analyzers are capable of handling 700 tests per hour with the capacity for processing urgent (STAT) samples along with the batch analysis, thus making critical information available quickly for emergency samples. 

The Stat Profile Phox Plus L is a compact, multiparametric and automated blood gas analyzer measuring blood gases, blood pH, electrolytes and lactate. The analytes are traced to the NIST Standard Reference Materials. 

The Biorad D-10 haemoglobin testing system measures HbA1c levels for the diabetes monitoring. The cation-exchange high performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) technology used for measurement is the reference method for haemoglobinopathy thus ensuring maximum accuracy. 



Immunoserology
The section of immunoserology offers an exhaustive menu of investigations covering hormones, vitamins, infectious diseases, oncology, cardiology and anemia. The immunodiagnostic Vitros ECiQ system provides marked improvement in quality of results, optimizing assay performance and productivity. The continuous, random access to the sample loading alongwith STAT operation helps in providing results any time. Various unique and patented technologies in the system perform, monitor and verify diagnostic checks throughout sample and assay processing resulting in increased confidence in result reporting. The enhanced chemiluminescence technology provides for accurate and highly specific results. 
The fully automated Abbott Architect i1000sr system is a fully automated immunoassay system allowing random and continuous access as well as priority and automated retest processing. The system has a diagnostic module with priority processing capability that performs sample processing using CMIA (Chemiluminescent Microparticle Immunoassay) method and capable of processing upto 100 tests per hour.
The automated multiparametric immunoassay analyzer, Vidas PC Blue, offers testing for serology, immunochemistry, antigen detection, industrial microbiology and immunochemostasis. The technology combines the EIA method with a final fluorescence reading; this technology is known as ELFA (Enzyme Linked Fluorescence Assay). 
The ELISA analyzers are used in the analysis of various hormones and serology markers. The analyzers facilitate accurate results to ensure good sensitivity & specificity. In addition to routine tests like Widal, VDRL, RA, CRP, etc serological investigations for "difficult to grow" pathogens as Leptospira, Dengue, Rotavirus etc. are performed by enzyme immunoassay and latex agglutination techniques.

Haematology
The fully automated 5 part differential haematology analyzers, Beckman Coulter LH 750 and Horiba ABX Pentra XL 80, perform complete haemogram, boosting of high-speed auto-sampling alongwith STAT sampling facility. These equipments have integrated validation station with comprehensive delta-check flagging helping generate interpretative data, thereby warning about abnormalities of blood cells. All such 'flagged' samples are then visualised by the morphologist to confirm diagnosis. The network laboratory configuration provides for positive sample identification and bidirectional interface. 
The compact ST art 4 coagulometer analyzer is a complete workstation for haemostasis. Apart from the routine coagulation tests such as prothrombin time and activated thromboplastin time, the factor studies, fibrinogen, protein C & S and lupus anticoagulant assays are performed. Thus, reliable monitoring of oral anticoagulation is made possible with the help of high quality reagents and analyzer.


Clinical Pathology
The Clinical Pathology section is equipment with Siemens Clinitech for the urine analysis. Various tests such as urine examination, stool examination, body fluids examination and semen analysis are conducted in this section.

Microbiology
The BacT Alert 3D Microbial Detection System is an automated test system capable of incubating, agitating and continuously monitoring aerobic and anaerobic media inoculated with patient specimens suspected of having bacteremia, fungemia and / or mycobacteremia. The BacT Alert MB configuration is designed to rapidly grow and detect mycobacteria from patient samples. The mini API is an automated instrument for the identification and susceptibility testing of the microorganisms. The antimicrobial susceptibility testing is performed by disc diffusion method using the CLSI (Clinical and Laboratory Standards Institute), formerly NCCLS. All the routine samples are examined for Gram’s organism and Acid Fast Bacilli. Apart from routine isolation & identification for aerobic organisms of all clinical samples, anaerobic, fungal & mycobacterial cultures are also done from the clinical samples.

Histopathology And Cytopathology
The section offers services for processing of clinical specimens for histopathology as well as reporting of fluids, smears and fine needle aspirations. In addition, 'frozen section' service is provided for urgent diagnosis of surgical cases. All surgical specimens are processed by standard histological technique and reported with diagnosis, grading and staging of tumours according to international guidelines. The section also handles both gynaecological and non-gynaecological specimens sent for cytology. The gynaecological 'PAP smears' is examined for early detection of cervix cancer. Fine needle Aspiration Cytology (FNAC) is also carried out for all palpable swellings in the body as the first line of investigative workup before a surgical biopsy.
